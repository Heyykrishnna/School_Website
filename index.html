The Aditya Birla Public School

The Aditya Birla Public School, Nagda was established as Grasim Vidya Mandir on 13th July 1970, under the aegis of Grasim Industries Ltd.
The school was set up by Mr. Ghanshyam Dasji Birla to fulfill the need of quality education for the children of Grasim employees and the surrounding locality. The school is located at Nagda, (Ujjain district, M.P.) on the Delhi – Mumbai Train route.
The Aditya Birla Public School is a co-educational and a day boarding CBSE affiliated, ISO certified: 9001 -2015, school. The school has been rapturously enlarging since its inception and was later rechristened as The Aditya Birla Public School in the year 2003. It is one of the well-known CBSE schools in MP. With a rich legacy of over 50 years, the school exemplifies exuberance and vitality.
It aspires to dispense a holistic learning domain whereby the learners/ students/ pupils make headway to subtle virtues/attributes of the cognitive, affective and psychomotor faculties making them cosmopolitan.

About Arunodaya:



A school magazine is an annual publication that typically contains literary works, articles, and other information related to a school. Here are some key points about school magazines:





Purpose and Content:

School magazines serve as a platform for students to showcase their creativity and talents.

They include student-written essays, poems, stories, artwork, and photographs.

Articles may cover topics like school events, achievements, interviews, and educational insights

Publication Frequency:

School magazines are typically published once a year.

Students eagerly await the release of the magazine, which becomes a cherished keepsake.

Student Contributions:

Students actively participate by submitting their work to the magazine committee.

The magazine features a mix of literary genres, reflecting the diverse interests of the student body.

Themes and Topics:

Themes can vary, ranging from creativity and imagination to social issues and personal experiences.

Students explore different subjects, expressing their thoughts and perspectives.

Some school magazines have a rich history, dating back many years.
They become part of the school’s legacy and contribute to its sense of community.
